### Reader-Quality Gap Analysis (Jack's Deep-Dive Request)

Jack asked to go beyond format/structure and compare from a **reader's perspective**: how understandable, accessible, and self-explanatory is each email?

**R1. Title readability** — ActivePiece rewrites titles for laymen. Our pipeline uses raw source titles or LLM with no accessibility guidance. Fix: Prompt must say "Rewrite titles so a non-expert instantly understands the topic"

**R2. Summary self-contained** — ActivePiece's 2 sentences explain WHAT + WHY IT MATTERS with zero assumed knowledge. Our summaries assume background (e.g., "HBM Market soars" — what's HBM?). Fix: "Each summary must explain what happened AND why it matters, with zero assumed knowledge"

**R3. Significance integrated** — ActivePiece builds significance into 2nd sentence. We bolt it on or skip it. Fix: Restructure to Sentence 1 = what happened, Sentence 2 = why it matters

**R4. Reader persona defined** — ActivePiece writes for a business-savvy founder who isn't an ML engineer. Our pipeline writes for nobody specific. Fix: Define persona: "Write for a tech-curious business founder who doesn't write code"

**R5. Article selection quality** — ActivePiece picks real-world impact stories (chips, military, policy, music industry). We pick HuggingFace blog posts and model release notes. Fix: "Prioritize articles about real-world impact over model release notes and tool launches"

**R6. Section framing purpose** — ActivePiece: "News Relevant to Local AI Startup" frames WHY section exists. We: "Relevant to Penang AI Startup" — narrower. Fix: Widen to "News Relevant to Local AI Startup"

**R7. Local section bridging** — ActivePiece picks a global story and weaves in local relevance naturally. We pick article + add generic tie-in ("this matters because AI is growing"). Fix: "Explain specifically how it affects a local AI startup's strategy, market, or opportunities"

**R8. Jargon avoidance** — ActivePiece explains technical terms in context. We use HBM, TFLOPS, CLDN18.2 without explanation. Fix: "If you use a technical term, briefly explain it in plain English within the sentence"
